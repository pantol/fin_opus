## Evening 6 — Monday 2026-07-20, 19:30 (sandbox)

**Settle:** Friday's two SELL orders filled at the Monday open — the first
realized (paper) losses:

| Fill | Qty | Exit price | Fee | Round trip |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| EXIT PEO | 82 | 229.44 | 71.49 | **−969 PLN** (entry 239.48 + both fees) |
| EXIT ALR | 1 | 133.03 | 3.00 | **−16 PLN** (entry 143.19 + both fees) |

Total realized ≈ **−985 PLN ≈ 1% of the book** — almost exactly the
`risk_per_trade` budget the sizing was built around. The risk layer's
promise ("a single loser costs ~1%") held in practice.

**The whipsaw, in full daylight:** the system sold at the open — and both
banks closed the day *higher* (PEO 232.50, ALR 137.95, both back above
their old stops). The morning intraday monitor had flagged exactly that
bounce at 10:28. The EOD contract sells anyway: the decision was Friday's
close below the stop, and the backtest prices this exact behavior — no
regret, no exceptions. This is the honest cost of trailing-stop discipline.

**Decide (Monday close):** both names immediately re-qualify (close >
SMA200, positive 6M momentum) → **BUY PEO 67 (stop 218.05)** and **BUY ALR
27 (stop 128.36)**, pending for the Tuesday open. Two details worth
noting: ALR re-enters with 27 shares, not 1 — selling PEO freed
banking-sector headroom at sizing time; and the fresh stops sit ~6% below
Monday's close, so the re-entered positions have full breathing room again.

**Mark-to-market:** equity **97 731.10 PLN** (+0.16% on a day WIG20TR rose
+0.47% — the drag is the open-price exits plus fees), cash 23 975.68, 6
open positions. Since inception: −2.27% vs benchmark −0.91%. Trailing
stops ratcheted again: PKN 138.07 → 138.57, OPL 13.48 → 13.57. **PKO
survived by a hair**: close 106.74 vs stop 106.34.
